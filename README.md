## 📖 Biography
Most people see software as a collection of functions. I see it as a complex landscape of memory addresses, syscalls, and privilege boundaries. My journey began with a simple curiosity: what happens when you look past the edge of the user space — directly into the heart of the system.

Under the brand **devkcore**, I dedicate my work to the art of **offensive security**. I don't operate on the surface; I dive into the deepest layers of the OS. For me, the kernel isn't an abstract concept, it’s pure, raw reality. 

Every exploit, every analysis, and every line of code follows my core principle: 
**observe → analyze → refine**. 

I don't just hunt for bugs; I study logical flaws at the hardware-software interface. If you're looking for me, you'll find me deep in a hex dump, somewhere between an ELF header and a task structure.
